# Empty States: Free and Community-Supported Approach

This document describes alternate empty states for the "Free and Community-Supported" onboarding approach. The core idea is that Soonlist is free forever, sustained by its community rather than investors, ads, or data harvesting. The voice is transparent, genuine, community-oriented, and anti-corporate. Supporting Soonlist is framed as joining a community, not buying a product.

Modeled after:
- **Signal** -- community sustainer badges, open about costs
- **Overcast** -- patronage model, all features free
- **Wikipedia** -- donation math ("if everyone reading this gave $3...")
- **Mastodon** -- "not for sale," community-owned infrastructure
- **Halide** -- indie team, personal tone, craft-first

Voice principles:
- "All features, always free. No ads. No tracking. Supported by the community."
- "Made by people, not a corporation."
- Supporting is joining, not purchasing.
- Skip buttons are prominent and guilt-free.
- No feature gating. No artificial limits. No "unlock" language.

## My List Empty State

Shown on the "My List" / "My Soonlist" tab when the user has no saved events.

| Element | Content |
|---------|---------|
| **Headline** | Your calendar, your way |
| **Subtitle** | Paste a link, share a screenshot, or type a few words -- Soonlist turns anything into an event. Every feature is free, forever. No catch. |
| **Visual** | A warm, full-color illustration of diverse event sources flowing into a single, clean event card. Sources include a concert poster, a group chat screenshot, a flyer on a telephone pole, and a handwritten note. The event card that emerges is bright, complete, and alive -- not ghosted or grayed out. The feeling is possibility, not emptiness. |
| **CTA Button** | "Add your first event" |
| **Secondary CTA** | Small text link below: "See how it works" (opens a brief inline walkthrough) |
| **Design Notes** | No grayscale source icons. No ghost card placeholders. The current empty state uses faded, desaturated imagery that signals "locked" or "disabled" even though nothing is locked. This version uses full-color, inviting art. The tone is welcoming, not transactional. There is no mention of limits, trials, or upgrades anywhere on this screen. A small footer line reads: "Soonlist is free and community-supported. No ads. No tracking." |

## Paywall / Support Empty State

This is the subscription ask, completely reframed. **This section is the most important for this approach because the paywall reframe IS the approach's identity.** There is no paywall. There is a support page.

| Element | Content |
|---------|---------|
| **Headline** | Support Soonlist |
| **Subtitle** | Soonlist is free for everyone, forever. No ads. No tracking. No investors demanding growth at all costs. We're supported by people like you. If Soonlist is useful to you, consider chipping in. |
| **Visual** | A hand-drawn-style illustration of a small, diverse group of people building something together -- raising a barn, tending a garden, or assembling a structure. The Soonlist logo is subtly woven into what they're building. The art style is warm, human, slightly imperfect on purpose. No stock-photo energy. No corporate polish. |
| **Transparency Block** | A short, Wikipedia-style transparency statement: "Soonlist costs about $X/month to run. Right now, Y community supporters keep it going. Every contribution matters." (Numbers are real and updated periodically.) |
| **Tier 1** | **"Buy us a coffee"** -- $4.99/month. "A small, steady way to keep Soonlist running. You'll get a supporter badge on your profile." |
| **Tier 2** | **"Champion"** -- $29.99/year. "Our biggest supporters. You'll get a champion badge, early access to new features, and a direct line to the team." |
| **Badge Preview** | A small visual showing what the supporter/champion badge looks like on a profile, modeled after Signal's sustainer badge -- subtle, tasteful, a mark of community membership rather than a status symbol. |
| **Skip Button** | Large, clearly styled, not buried or grayed out: **"Not now -- I'll use Soonlist for free"** |
| **Below Skip** | "Soonlist is fully functional without supporting. This will always be true. You can come back to this page anytime from Settings." |
| **Design Notes** | The skip button must be visually equal to or nearly as prominent as the support tiers. There is zero guilt language. No "You'll miss out." No "Are you sure?" confirmation. No countdown timer. No "limited time" framing. The entire screen reads like a letter from the team, not a conversion funnel. The word "unlock" never appears. The word "premium" never appears. The phrase "free trial" never appears. Everything the user can do today, they can do tomorrow, whether they support or not. The badges are the only tangible difference, and they are cosmetic. Early access for Champions means they see beta features sooner, not that features are withheld from free users. |

## Paywall Comparison: Current vs. Free and Community-Supported

| Dimension | Current Paywall | Free and Community-Supported |
|-----------|----------------|------------------------------|
| **Framing** | "Unlock Soonlist" | "Support Soonlist" |
| **Mental model** | Freemium product with gated features | Free tool sustained by community patronage |
| **Monthly price** | $9.99/month | $4.99/month ("Buy us a coffee") |
| **Yearly price** | $59.99/year | $29.99/year ("Champion") |
| **Free tier** | 3 events, then hard paywall | All features, no limits, forever |
| **Skip button** | Small, easy to miss, "Try 3 events free" | Large, prominent, "Not now -- I'll use Soonlist for free" |
| **Skip button tone** | Transactional, implies a countdown | Guilt-free, affirms that free is a real option |
| **Feature gating** | Yes -- features locked behind subscription | No -- all features available to all users |
| **What supporters get** | Access to features they've already seen | Badges, early beta access, community identity |
| **Guilt mechanics** | Implicit ("your trial is ending") | None by design |
| **Language** | "Unlock," "premium," "upgrade," "trial" | "Support," "community," "chip in," "keep it running" |
| **Transparency** | None -- pricing feels arbitrary | Real cost numbers, real supporter count |
| **Tone** | Corporate SaaS | Indie, human, community letter |
| **Re-engagement** | Push notifications about expiring trial | Occasional, gentle reminders in Settings; never interruptive |
| **Revenue model risk** | Higher per-user revenue, but churn and resentment | Lower per-user revenue, but loyalty, word-of-mouth, and community identity |
| **Comparable apps** | Most iOS subscription apps | Signal, Overcast, Wikipedia, Mastodon, Halide |

## Summary: Comparison to Current Empty States

### My List

The current My List empty state uses grayscale source icons (Instagram, TikTok, etc.) and ghost event card placeholders. The visual language -- desaturated, skeletal, placeholder-heavy -- unintentionally signals "this is a demo" or "pay to activate." The Free and Community-Supported version replaces this with full-color, inviting art and copy that emphasizes everything is already available. The empty state feels like an invitation, not a storefront.

### Board/Radar

The current Board/Radar empty state says "Events other people have captured will appear here" with an "Invite friends" button. It is functional but passive. The Free and Community-Supported version reframes the empty board as the beginning of a community relationship, not a blank page. The addition of an "Explore community events" link ensures the screen is never truly empty, and the language ("Better together," "yours is just getting started") positions the user as joining something, not waiting for something.

### Paywall/Support

This is where the two approaches diverge most. The current paywall is a RevenueCat-powered subscription gate: $9.99/month or $59.99/year, with a small "Try 3 events free" skip option. It follows the standard iOS freemium playbook. The Free and Community-Supported version dismantles this entirely. There is no gate. The support page is a community letter with real cost transparency, lower price points, cosmetic-only rewards (badges), and a skip button that is prominent, guilt-free, and permanent. The bet is that users who feel respected and un-pressured will support at higher rates over time, and will become evangelists rather than churning subscribers.

### Overall

The Free and Community-Supported approach treats empty states not as conversion funnels but as the user's first experience of the app's values. Every empty state reinforces the same message: this is free, this is yours, and the community is what makes it work.
