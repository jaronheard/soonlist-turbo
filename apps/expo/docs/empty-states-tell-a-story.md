# Empty States: "Tell a Story" Onboarding Approach

This document describes alternate empty states following a narrative-driven onboarding approach. The central character is **Maya** -- 28, lives in a mid-size city, loves going to things but keeps missing them. Her story arc moves through four beats: **chaos** (missed events, screenshots lost in camera rolls), **discovery** (finds Soonlist), **organization** (captures events effortlessly), and **resolution** (actually goes, and it changes how she experiences her city).

This approach is modeled after:

- **Headspace** -- Andy's guided meditation journey, where the user feels personally shepherded
- **Florence** -- an interactive wordless story where the user *participates* in the narrative
- **Finch** -- hatching and nurturing a character, building emotional investment over time
- **Duolingo** -- a mascot with genuine personality who reacts to your behavior

**Voice and tone:** Warm, narrative, personal, storybook-like. Maya is not a mascot who lectures -- she is a relatable stand-in. The user is stepping into her shoes, and the empty states mark the opening pages of their own story. An empty list is not a failure state; it is "Chapter 1."

---

## My List Empty State

Shown on the **"My List" / "My Soonlist"** tab when the user has zero saved events.

| Element | Content |
|---|---|
| **Headline** | Chapter 1: The Blank Page |
| **Subtitle** | Maya used to have a camera roll full of screenshots she never looked at again. Flyers she forgot. DMs she meant to revisit. Then she saved her first event here -- and everything changed. This is where your story starts. |
| **Visual description** | A soft, illustrated storybook spread. On the left page, a faded montage of chaos: overlapping screenshots, a clock with spinning hands, a concert poster crumpled at the edges. On the right page, a single clean event card glowing gently -- like a door just opened. The pages have a slight paper texture. Warm color palette (cream, soft gold, muted coral). |
| **CTA button copy** | Save your first event |
| **Design notes** | The storybook metaphor should feel tactile -- think slightly rounded corners on the "pages," a faint spine shadow down the center. The chaos side should not feel stressful, just relatable and slightly humorous. The clean event card on the right should pulse subtly once, like a heartbeat, to draw the eye. Below the CTA, a secondary text link reads: "Or paste a link, screenshot, or text -- we'll handle the rest." The overall feeling is an invitation, not an instruction. |

## Board / Radar Empty State

Shown on the **"Board" / "Community Board" / "Radar"** tab when the user is not following anyone and no community events are visible.

| Element | Content |
|---|---|
| **Headline** | Chapter 2: The People Around You |
| **Subtitle** | Maya went to things alone for years. Good things, but alone. Then she started seeing what her friends were saving -- a comedy show she almost missed, a market she never knew about. Her city got bigger. Yours can too. |
| **Visual description** | An illustrated bird's-eye view of a city block at golden hour. Small, warmly drawn figures are walking toward different venues -- a lit-up doorway, a park with string lights, a rooftop with tiny silhouettes. Faint dotted paths connect the figures to each other, suggesting a network. One figure in the foreground (subtly Maya-like but ambiguous enough to be "you") stands at a crossroads, looking out at all the possibilities. |
| **CTA button copy** | Find people to follow |
| **Design notes** | The illustration should feel alive but not busy -- more Miyazaki background than Where's Waldo. The dotted-path motif ties back to the idea of connection without being literal social-graph imagery. A secondary action below the CTA reads: "Invite a friend and see what they're going to." The empty state should not make the user feel lonely; it should make them feel like they are about to discover something. The city should feel like *their* city -- warm, mid-size, walkable. |

## Paywall / Support Empty State

The subscription ask, reframed to match the narrative tone. Shown when the user hits the upgrade prompt (after free tier usage or as a soft gate).

| Element | Content |
|---|---|
| **Headline** | Keep the Story Going |
| **Subtitle** | Maya almost went back to screenshots and forgotten DMs. But she kept going -- and last month she went to eleven things she would have missed. Soonlist is built by a small team that believes you should actually go to the things you want to go to. Supporting us keeps this story alive -- yours and ours. |
| **Visual description** | A single illustrated page turning, mid-flip, as if the book is continuing. On the visible page, a timeline of small, joyful moments: Maya at a concert with her hands up, Maya laughing at a farmers market, Maya and a friend clinking glasses on a rooftop. The turning page reveals a soft glow -- the next chapter, unwritten, waiting. Below the illustration, the pricing options are presented on what looks like a bookmark or ticket stub. |
| **CTA button copy** | Continue the story -- $9.99/mo |
| **Secondary CTA** | Best value: $59.99/year (save 50%) |
| **Skip/dismiss copy** | Try 3 events free and decide later |
| **Design notes** | This is the most emotionally important empty state. It must not feel transactional. The illustration does the heavy lifting -- the *feeling* of momentum and joy should make the ask feel natural, not interrupting. The pricing should be understated, presented on the "bookmark" element so it feels like part of the story rather than a modal paywall. The skip option should be genuinely visible and guilt-free; the narrative approach depends on trust, and a hostile paywall would break the spell. The phrase "yours and ours" is intentional -- it frames the subscription as mutual support, not a gate. |

## Summary: Current vs. "Tell a Story" Empty States

| Context | Current Approach | "Tell a Story" Approach |
|---|---|---|
| **My List** | "Turn screenshots into possibilities" -- grayscale source icons (Instagram, TikTok, etc.) and ghost event card placeholders. Functional, benefit-driven, slightly abstract. | "Chapter 1: The Blank Page" -- illustrated storybook spread showing Maya's chaos-to-clarity arc. The empty state *is* the beginning of a story, not a prompt to fill a void. Emotional, narrative, personal. |
| **Board / Radar** | "Events other people have captured will appear here" -- "Invite friends" button. Informational, passive, tells the user what *will* happen but does not make them feel anything about it. | "Chapter 2: The People Around You" -- illustrated golden-hour cityscape with connected figures. Frames community not as a feature but as a turning point in Maya's story (and yours). Aspirational, warm. |
| **Paywall / Support** | RevenueCat default with monthly ($9.99) and yearly ($59.99), "Try 3 events free" skip. Standard, transactional, no narrative framing. | "Keep the Story Going" -- page-turning illustration with joyful moments, pricing on a bookmark element, mutual-support framing. Same price points, completely different emotional register. The ask feels like a continuation, not an interruption. |

The key shift across all three: current empty states describe **what the product does**. "Tell a Story" empty states describe **what the user's life could feel like**. Maya's story is the bridge -- she makes the abstract concrete and the functional emotional. The user is not onboarding into an app; they are picking up where Maya left off. "That's Maya's story. Now let's start yours."
